Heritage

We started with one shirt pattern and a stubborn idea: clothes should be built, not manufactured.

Since 1986, Ravelo has kept to the same principles — oxford cloth that softens with age, buttons sewn to stay, seams that hold up to actual wear. The silhouettes borrow from the field, the stable, and the university common room: unfussy, generous in the right places, cut to move.

We don't chase seasons. Most of what we make today was already correct forty years ago, and we expect it to still be correct forty years from now.

Craft

Built the slow way.

Three decisions we don't compromise on, no matter what it costs us in speed.

01

Natural fiber, always

Long-staple cotton, Shetland wool, waxed canvas. No synthetic blends looking to cut costs at the expense of how a garment ages.

02

Cut for movement

Patterns drafted with room in the shoulder and a true waist, so a jacket still fits the way you actually stand and reach.

03

Stitched to be repaired

Reinforced seams and spare buttons sewn into every hem, because a piece worth keeping is a piece worth mending.

Stay in the loop

New arrivals, twice a year. Nothing else.
